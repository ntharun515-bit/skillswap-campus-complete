Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using SkillSwap. Can't find your answer? Reach out to us.

🚀 Getting Started

What is SkillSwap? ▼
SkillSwap is a closed, university-exclusive freelance marketplace. Students can offer their skills (coding, design, writing, tutoring) as services, and other students or faculty can hire them. All transactions are protected by our virtual credit escrow system.
Who can register? ▼
Anyone with a valid university email or campus affiliation can register. You choose your role during registration: Student (freelancer) — offer your skills and apply to projects, or Client — post projects and hire talent.
Is it free to use? ▼
Yes, SkillSwap is completely free with zero platform fees. Unlike traditional freelancing sites that take 20%+ commission, we don't deduct anything from your earnings. The platform runs on virtual campus credits.
How do I complete my profile? ▼
Navigate to Settings in your dashboard. Add your full name, campus, upload an avatar, write a bio, add your skills, and optionally link your GitHub/LinkedIn profiles. A complete profile gets more visibility and AI match recommendations.

💼 Projects & Hiring

How do I post a project? ▼
As a client, go to Post Project in the sidebar. Fill in the title, description, required skills, budget (in campus credits), and deadline. Once published, student freelancers can discover and apply to your project.
How do I apply to a project? ▼
As a student, browse the Projects page or check your AI Matches on the dashboard. Click "Apply" on any project, write a cover letter explaining your approach, and optionally propose a custom rate. The client will review your application.
What happens after a client accepts my application? ▼
When accepted: 1) The project budget is deducted from the client's wallet and locked in escrow. 2) The project status moves to "In Progress." 3) Both parties gain access to the Collaborative Workspace with Kanban boards, real-time chat, file sharing, and timeline tracking. 4) All other applicants are automatically notified as "Not Selected."
How do I submit my completed work? ▼
Inside the Workspace, use the Submit Deliverables panel. Paste your deliverable URL (GitHub repo, Vercel deploy, Figma link, etc.) and write a handoff note. This changes the project status to "Submitted" and notifies the client to review your work.

💳 Payments & Escrow

How does the escrow system work? ▼
When a client hires a freelancer, the full project budget is instantly moved from their wallet into a locked escrow state. Neither party can access these funds. The credits are only released to the freelancer when the client approves the submitted work — or refunded to the client if an admin resolves a dispute in their favor.
How do I add credits to my wallet? ▼
Go to Settings → Wallet and enter a deposit amount. In this campus simulation, credits are added instantly. In a production environment, this would connect to a real payment gateway (Stripe, PayPal, etc.).
What if the client doesn't approve my work? ▼
The client has two options: 1) Request Revision — sends the project back to "In Progress" with specific notes. 2) Open Dispute — files a formal dispute with evidence for admin mediation. Escrowed funds remain locked until the dispute is resolved.
Are there any fees? ▼
No. SkillSwap charges zero commission on transactions. 100% of the project budget goes to the freelancer upon work approval. This is a key differentiator from traditional platforms.

🖥️ Workspace & Collaboration

What is the Collaborative Workspace? ▼
Each hired project gets a shared workspace that includes: Kanban Task Board (To Do → In Progress → Review → Done), Real-Time Project Chat with typing indicators, File & Deliverable Drawer for sharing links, Project Timeline tracking all events, Escrow Status Card, and a Submit/Review Panel.
Can I message the other party before hiring? ▼
Yes. Use the Messages page in your dashboard to start a conversation with any user. The workspace chat is specifically for project-related communication after hiring.

🏆 Verification & Reputation

How does skill verification work? ▼
Go to Career & AI Hub → Skill Verification. Choose a topic (JavaScript, Python, UI/UX, etc.) and take a timed quiz. Score 80% or higher to earn the Verified Pro badge, +250 XP, and increased visibility in search results.
What are XP, levels, and badges? ▼
SkillSwap uses a gamified reputation system. Earn XP by completing projects, verifying skills, and maintaining streaks. As XP grows, you level up: Rookie → Skilled → Pro Freelancer → Campus Expert. Badges are unlocked for specific achievements like first completed project, high ratings, or passing verification exams.
How are reviews managed? ▼
After a project is completed, both the client and freelancer can leave reviews with a 1–5 star rating and written feedback. Reviews are public and directly affect the freelancer's rating average, which influences search ranking and AI match scores.
